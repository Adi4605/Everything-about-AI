The basic computational unit in an artificial neural network, modeled after biological neurons to process information by receiving inputs, applying weights, summing them, and passing the result through an activation function to produce an output, mimicking how the brain learns by adjusting these synaptic weights to recognize patterns.

Key Components & Function
- **Inputs:** Data signals (usually numbers) received from other neurons or external sources. 
- **Weights:** Each input has an associated weight, representing its importance, which is adjusted during learning. 
- **Summation:** Inputs are multiplied by their weights and summed up. 
- **Bias:** An additional value added to the weighted sum, helping to shift the activation function. 
- **[[Activation Function]]** A non-linear function (like Sigmoid, ReLU) that determines the neuron's output, deciding whether and how strongly it "fires". 
- **Output:** The final result, passed to other neurons in the network.
